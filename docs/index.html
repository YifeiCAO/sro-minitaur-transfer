行为基础模型 × 个体差异 Behavioral Foundation Models × Individual Differences

你在一个任务上的行为,能预测你在另一个任务上的行为吗?

Does how you behave on one task predict how you behave on another?

我们微调一个行为基础模型(Minitaur = Llama-3.1-8B + Psych-101),让它读一个人在任务 A 上逐试次的选择,去预测同一个人在任务 B 上的选择 —— 基于 Self-Regulation Ontology(522 人 · 11 个认知任务)。核心问题:基础模型捕捉到的"认知指纹",能不能跨任务迁移?

We fine-tune a behavioral foundation model (Minitaur = Llama-3.1-8B + Psych-101) to read a person's trial-by-trial choices on task A and predict the same person's choices on task B — across the Self-Regulation Ontology (522 people · 11 cognitive tasks). The question: does the "cognitive fingerprint" a foundation model captures transfer across tasks?

研究问题

The question

从"手工总结分数"到"模型读原始行为"

From hand-crafted summary scores to a model reading raw behavior

个体差异通常用手工设计的总结量(一个折扣率、一个 d′)来刻画。我们换个问法:一个在原始人类行为上训练出来的基础模型,是否在它的内部状态里携带了一个人的认知特征,并且这种特征能跨任务迁移?更关键的是 —— 这种迁移是真实的认知过程,还是仅仅反映了一个人稳定的选择偏好?

Individual differences are usually captured with hand-crafted summary scores (a discount rate, a d′). We ask it differently: does a foundation model trained on raw human behavior carry a person's cognitive signature in a way that transfers across tasks — and crucially, is that transfer a genuine cognitive process, or merely a person's stable response bias?

方法 — 教模型读"前一个任务"。Method — teach the model to read the prior task. ①训练一个群体模型 M_pop;②在拼接的 [任务A + 任务B] 序列上继续微调,让模型学会以前一个任务的行为为条件;③测试时,对留出被试,用他自己的 A 记录 vs 一个随机陌生人的 A 记录,分别给他的 B 选择打分。 (1) train a population model M_pop; (2) further fine-tune on concatenated [task-A + task-B] sequences so the model learns to condition on prior-task behavior; (3) at test, for a held-out person, score their task-B choices given their own task-A transcript vs a random stranger's.
判据 — 置乱对照。The test — the shuffled control. 如果"用自己的 A"比"用陌生人的 A"更能预测自己的 B(更低的 NLL),那就是个体特异的跨任务迁移,而不只是模型变聪明了。 If your own A predicts your B better (lower NLL) than a stranger's A does, the signal is person-specific cross-task transfer — not just a smarter model.
💡 核心洞察:为什么个体信号"天生很薄"
💡 Key idea: why the individual signal is thin by design

因为模型只在选择上算损失,它的隐状态编码的是群体在给定刺激下的预期选择 —— 这在人与人之间是共享的、与个体无关。个体只活在残差里:模型对你实际选择的惊讶程度。所以可迁移的信号本来就薄(反应时对它不可见)—— 而这恰恰是这项研究的科学看点。

Because the model is trained only on choices, its hidden states encode the population's expected choice given the stimulus — shared across people, person-invariant. The individual lives in the residual: how surprised the model is by your actual choices. So the transferable signal is thin by construction (reaction time is invisible to it) — which is exactly the scientific point.

  • 基准率对照(最大风险):自己的 A 可能只泄漏了你的选择率。对照"选择率最接近的陌生人",若 real 仍更优 ⇒ 是迁移不是偏好。进行中
  • Base-rate control (biggest risk): own-A might just leak your choice rate. Compare against the marginal-matched stranger; if own still wins ⇒ transfer, not bias. In progress

  • Statistic: replace the inflated t-test with a permutation null + bootstrap CI. Implemented
  • 域内 ≫ 跨域:全矩阵 + FDR 校正,把"条件作用"升级为"域特异迁移"。进行中

⚖️ 诚实评估
⚖️ Honest read

效应小但方向极稳(73–85% 的人都成立)。这与一个核心论点一致:个体可迁移信号的很大一部分由反应时承载,而一个仅选择的模型看不见它。

The effect is small but rock-solid in direction (73–85% of people). This fits a core thesis: much of the transferable individual signal is carried by reaction time, which a choice-only model cannot see.

今天能站得住的最强表述:在同一个认知域内,用一个人自己的前序任务作条件,会比用陌生人的更能降低他下一个任务选择的 NLL。补上基准率对照与"域内>跨域",即可升级为"迁移"。

Strongest defensible claim today: within a cognitive domain, conditioning the model on a person's own prior session lowers their next-task choice NLL more than a stranger's does. The base-rate control + within>across upgrade this to "transfer".
