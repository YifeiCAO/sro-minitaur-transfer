行为基础模型中的跨任务个体差异迁移

Cross-task Individual Transfer in a Behavioral Foundation Model

一个人在任务 A 上逐试次的选择,能否预测他在任务 B 上的选择?

Can a person's trial-by-trial choices on one task predict their choices on another?

摘要

Abstract

个体差异通常用手工设计的总结量(折扣率、d′)来刻画。我们改为考察:一个在原始人类行为上训练的基础模型(Minitaur = Llama-3.1-8B + Psych-101),其内部是否携带可跨任务迁移的个体认知特征。基于 Self-Regulation Ontology(522 名被试,11 个认知任务),我们在拼接的 [任务A + 任务B] 序列上微调模型,并以置乱对照检验个体特异性:用一个人自己的前序任务作条件,是否比用陌生人的更能预测他的下一个任务。个体特异迁移**在 9 对中的 7 对扛过了"选择率匹配"对照**(即超出选择偏好的真信号),且是**域特异的**(域内 id 约 0.20,跨域 ≈ 随机 0.10)。最干净的格子是均衡任务(CCT 冷↔热 −0.02, p=5e-5;冲突 DF/DPX↔近探针);饱和的折扣任务里残差很小。换言之:**可迁移的个体过程信号真实存在、但很薄,且似乎集中在"选择不饱和"的任务上**(提示性)。

Individual differences are usually captured with hand-crafted summary scores (a discount rate, a d′). We instead ask whether a foundation model trained on raw human behavior (Minitaur = Llama-3.1-8B + Psych-101) carries an individual's cognitive signature in a way that transfers across tasks. On the Self-Regulation Ontology (522 subjects, 11 cognitive tasks), we fine-tune the model on concatenated [task-A + task-B] sequences and test person-specificity with a shuffled control: does conditioning on a person's own prior task predict their next task better than a stranger's? Person-specific transfer survives a marginal-matched base-rate control in 7 of 9 tested pairs (i.e. real signal beyond choice rate) and is domain-specific (within-domain id ≈ 0.20, across-domain ≈ chance 0.10). The cleanest cells are choice-balanced tasks (CCT cold↔hot −0.02, p=5e-5; conflict DF/DPX↔recent); in saturated discounting tasks the residual is tiny. In short: a transferable individual process signal is real but thin, and appears concentrated in non-saturated tasks (suggestive).

Data. Self-Regulation Ontology (Eisenberg & Poldrack, 2019): 522 subjects, 11 tasks across 4 domains — delay discounting, risky choice, reinforcement learning, and conflict/memory-control — each rendered as natural-language trial-by-trial transcripts. Split 418 train / 104 held-out.

2方法Method

  • 群体模型 M_pop。先做群体层面的微调,得到一个不区分个体的基线。
  • Population model M_pop. First a population-level fine-tune, giving a person-agnostic baseline.
  • 上下文微调。在拼接序列 [任务A 全程 + 任务B 全程] 上继续微调(损失只在 B 的选择上),教模型学会以前一个任务的行为为条件。
  • In-context fine-tune. Continue fine-tuning on concatenated [full task-A + full task-B] sequences (loss only on B's choices), teaching the model to condition on prior-task behavior.
  • 置乱对照(判据)。对每个留出被试,用他自己的 A 记录 vs 一个随机陌生人的 A 记录,分别给他的 B 选择打分。自己 < 陌生人(更低 NLL)即为个体特异迁移。
  • Shuffled control (the test). For each held-out person, score their B choices given their own A transcript vs a random stranger's. own < stranger (lower NLL) indicates person-specific transfer.

为什么个体信号天生很薄

Why the individual signal is thin by design

因为模型只在选择上算损失,其隐状态编码的是"群体在给定刺激下的预期选择"——人际共享、与个体无关。个体只活在残差里:模型对你实际选择的惊讶程度。因此可迁移的信号本就有限(反应时对它不可见),这正是本研究的看点。

Because the model is trained only on choices, its hidden states encode the population's expected choice given the stimulus — shared across people, person-invariant. The individual lives in the residual: how surprised the model is by your actual choices. The transferable signal is therefore limited by construction (reaction time is invisible to it) — which is precisely the point of interest.

超出 base-rate,而且域特异

Beyond base-rate, and domain-specific

5局限与诚实评估Limitations

效应小但方向极稳(73–85% 的被试)。这与一个核心论点一致:可迁移的个体信号很大一部分由反应时承载,而仅选择的模型看不见它。今天能站得住的最强表述是:

The effect is small but rock-solid in direction (73–85% of people), consistent with the thesis that much of the transferable individual signal is carried by reaction time — invisible to a choice-only model. The strongest defensible claim today:

在同一认知域内,用一个人自己的前序任务作条件,会比用陌生人的更能降低其下一任务选择的 NLL。
Within a cognitive domain, conditioning the model on a person's own prior session lowers their next-task choice NLL more than a stranger's does.

补上基准率对照与"域内>跨域",即可将此表述升级为"迁移"。

The base-rate control and within>across upgrade this statement to "transfer".
